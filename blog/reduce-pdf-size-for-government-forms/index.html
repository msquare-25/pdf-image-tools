How to Reduce PDF Size for Government Forms (Without Image Resize)

Government portals often reject PDFs that exceed size limits. This guide shows how to reduce PDF size safely — without resizing images or damaging readability.

Why government portals reject PDF uploads

  • Strict size limits (100KB, 500KB, 1MB)
  • Scanned PDFs contain high-resolution images
  • Extra blank pages increase file size
  • Large margins waste space inside the PDF

Step 1: Compress the PDF (primary solution)

  1. Open Compress PDF.
  2. Upload your government form PDF.
  3. Select a balanced or strong compression preset.
  4. Download the compressed PDF.

Most portals accept PDFs below 1MB. Compression alone solves the majority of upload rejections.

Step 2: Remove unnecessary pages

  1. Open Delete pages.
  2. Remove blank or instruction pages.
  3. Keep only mandatory pages.
  4. Download the cleaned PDF.

Step 3: Crop extra margins (optional but effective)

  1. Use Crop PDF.
  2. Trim large white margins.
  3. Apply cropping to all pages.

Cropping does not resize images — it simply removes wasted space.

Common government forms with PDF size limits

  • UPSC, SSC, Banking exam uploads
  • Visa & passport applications
  • University admissions
  • Government job onboarding forms
