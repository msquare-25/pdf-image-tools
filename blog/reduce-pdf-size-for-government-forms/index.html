PDF Upload Failed Due to Size Limit? Fix “File size exceeds limit” (Online Forms)

If a portal rejects your PDF with messages like “File too large”, “Size exceeds limit”, or “Upload failed”, you can usually fix it in minutes without re-scanning — using browser-only tools.

Why portals reject PDFs (common reasons)

  • Scanned PDFs contain big image pages (largest size impact).
  • Unnecessary pages (blank pages, instructions, duplicates).
  • Huge white margins from scans increase pixel area and file size.
  • Multiple attachments merged into one oversized PDF.

Good news: most of this can be reduced without hurting readability.

Step-by-step: Fix the size limit (fast workflow)

Step 1: Compress the PDF (first attempt)

  1. Open Compress PDF.
  2. Upload your PDF.
  3. Choose a stronger preset (or expand Advanced if you know what you’re changing).
  4. Download and check the new file size.

Step 2: Delete useless pages (big wins)

  1. Open Delete pages.
  2. Remove blank pages, repeated pages, and any page not required by the portal.
  3. Download the cleaned PDF, then compress again if needed.

Step 3: Crop white margins (reduces scan area)

  1. Open Crop PDF.
  2. Crop to remove empty white borders (margins) around the content.
  3. Download the cropped PDF, then compress again if needed.

Practical tip: for strict limits (20KB/50KB/100KB), you often need Compress → Delete pages → Crop → Compress again.

If the portal limit is extremely strict (20KB / 50KB / 100KB)

  • Prioritize content: keep only required pages. Remove every non-essential page.
  • Crop aggressively: keep only the actual text/photo area, remove borders.
  • Avoid re-saving repeatedly in random apps (some apps bloat PDFs).
  • Check readability at 100% zoom before uploading (numbers and names must be clear).

If your PDF is photo-heavy (scans), size will drop more. If it’s mostly selectable text, compression may be limited.

Common upload errors and what they mean

  • “File size exceeds limit” → reduce PDF size using Compress PDF.
  • “Invalid PDF” → re-export once; avoid corrupted merges; try a fresh download from the source.
  • “Upload failed” → sometimes network; but many times file is too big or the portal is timing out.

Everything runs inside your browser — your file never uploads to our server.
