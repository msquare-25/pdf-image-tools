How to Fix Blurry or Low-Quality PDFs

Is your PDF text unclear, fuzzy, or hard to read after scanning or compressing? This guide shows practical ways to improve PDF clarity using free browser-based tools.

Why PDFs become blurry

  • Low-resolution scanning (DPI too low).
  • Aggressive PDF compression.
  • Images stretched beyond their original size.
  • Repeated PDF conversions.

Step 1 — Check if the PDF is image-based

Zoom into the PDF. If text pixels break when zoomed, it’s image-based. Scanned PDFs are essentially photos inside a PDF container.

Step 2 — Avoid over-compression

Compression saves size but can destroy clarity.

  1. Open Compress PDF.
  2. Select Balanced or High quality.
  3. Avoid “strong” compression unless file size is critical.

Step 3 — Rebuild the PDF from high-quality images

This is the most effective fix for blurry scanned PDFs.

  1. Convert the PDF using PDF → Images.
  2. Use PNG format for better clarity.
  3. Re-create the PDF using Image → PDF.
  4. Choose proper page size (A4) and margins.

Step 4 — Crop extra space to improve perceived sharpness

Extra borders make text appear smaller and blurrier.

  • Open Crop PDF.
  • Remove unnecessary margins.
  • Apply crop to all pages.

Step 5 — Avoid resizing PDFs after export

Stretching a PDF in viewers or editors degrades clarity. Always export at final size.

Best practices for clear PDFs

  • Scan at 300 DPI or higher.
  • Use PNG for scanned documents.
  • Compress only once.
  • Keep original files as backup.
